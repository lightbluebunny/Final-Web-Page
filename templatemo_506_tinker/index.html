The Hermit Kingdom: South Korea

Many people know the Korean War, but they do not know the country, South Korea. Despite the blind eye to the culture, South Korea is deeply routed in history and is well-known for their pop-culture.

History

Korea's history is only well-known within it's own country. It is one that represents the divisions between the North and South today and defines their connections to China.

The War

The Korean War was the war that showed the contrasting views of those who lived in the North and those in the South. This war left a permanent mark on their day to day life today.

Seoul, South Korea

The Capital

Seoul is probably one of the most diverse cities in the whole of South Korea. In Seoul, you can visit the shopping district, Gangnam, to the ancient houses, Bukchon Hanok Village. Seoul contains a diverse society full of people of all sorts. This town has the most amounth of foreigners than any other city in South Korea.

Jeju Island, South Korea

The Island

Jeju is probably one of the most well visited places by families in South Korea. Jeju has a variety of things to see and do like visitng the highest mountain in South Korea, going in lava tubs, and visiting the grandfather stones. Jeju also has their own type of tangerine called a hallabong, which is supposedly better than oranges and tangerines.

Busan, South Korea

The Sea City

Busan is a city by the sea with arguably the best seafood in the country. Busan has a sea port and a range of beaches to choose from. As well as being a city by the sea, it has the famous Beomeosa temple, which is 1,300 years old.

Pyeongchang, South Korea

Winter Olympics 2018

In 2018, Pyeongchang will be hosting the 2018 Winter Olympics. Pyeongchang has a well knwon ski restort to many Koreans.
